Sequential DLA

In this section, we analyze the results of running our DLA algorithm sequentially. Meaning that we release one random walker at a time, sequentially. There is only ever one free particle walking, so this is the more time-consuming approach.

Parallel DLA

In this section, we analyze the results of running our DLA algorithm in parallel using vectorization in MATLAB.

We cleverly take advantage of MATLAB's vectorization features and represent our random walker's positions in column vectors. Instead of adjusting a single random walker's \((x, y)\) position at a time, we have two vectors of size \(N_{particles}\) holding \((x, y)\) data of ALL random walkers simultaneously.
